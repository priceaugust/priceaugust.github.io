Our Team

Kimberly Walker

Chief Investment Officer
Kim is the Chief Investment Officer at Washington University Investment Management Company (WUIMC) and oversees the investment of the endowment and other assets which total over $7 billion. Her responsibilities include developing the endowment’s overall investment philosophy, establishing a strategic asset policy and selecting and managing outside investment managers. Kim joined WUIMC in 2006 after serving as President of Qwest Asset Management Company where she managed Qwest’s pension and other employee benefit assets. Prior to that position, Kim spent 13 years in various finance positions at General Motors, the last eight as an employee of GM’s New York-based investment management subsidiary, GMIMCo.
Kim is a board member of The Managed Funds Association and a director for Commerce Bancshares, Inc. She also serves on the board of the St. Louis Symphony endowment trust. Prior volunteer roles include serving as: Chair of the Committee on Investment of Employee Benefit Assets (CIEBA) and board member of its parent organization, the Association of Financial Professionals; member of the New York Stock Exchange Pension Managers’ Advisory Committee; a member of the University of Colorado Investment Advisory Committee; and Chair of the Miami University Foundation Board. Kim earned a bachelor’s degree in economics and public administration from Miami University, a master’s degree in economics from Washington University in St. Louis, and a master’s degree in business administration from The University of Michigan. Kim holds the Chartered Financial Analyst designation.

Pamela Campbell

Managing Director of Hedged Strategies
Pam is responsible for the research, sourcing, due diligence, manager selection, portfolio construction and overall strategy of the hedge fund investments for the WUIMC portfolio. Pam joined WUIMC in 1997 from McDonnell Douglas where she was a manager in the investment department which oversaw the investment of the pension and defined benefit plans. Pam is a member of the Donald Danforth Plant Science Investment Committee, the Investment Committee for the YMCA of Greater St. Louis and on the Board, Investment Committee and Health Policy Committee of the Missouri Foundation for Health. Pam earned a bachelor’s degree in psychology from the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign and a master’s degree in business administration from Saint Louis University. Pam holds the Chartered Alternative Investment Analyst designation.

Daniel Feder

Managing Director of Private Markets
Dan is responsible for the research, sourcing, due diligence, manager selection, portfolio construction and overall strategy of the private market asset classes which include private equity, venture capital, real estate and natural resources. Dan joined WUIMC in 2013 from TIAA-CREF where he served as Senior Investment Manager. Prior to that position, Dan served as Managing Director and Advisor to the Heritage Fund for Sequoia Capital. Dan was previously a Managing Director at Princeton University Investment Company and was a Portfolio Manager at CalPERS. Dan began his career as an attorney working at the law firm of Hebb & Gitlin and at Cigna. Dan is a member of the UMass Foundation Investment Committee and serves on the Dean’s Advisory Board for the School of Social & Behavioral Sciences at UMass. Dan earned a bachelor’s degree in political science from University of Massachusetts, a juris doctoris from Boston University School of Law and a master’s degree in economics from Trinity College-Hartford. Dan holds the Chartered Financial Analyst designation.

Mark Newcomb

Mark is responsible for the research, sourcing, due diligence, manager selection, portfolio construction and overall strategy of the public market investments for the WUIMC portfolio.
